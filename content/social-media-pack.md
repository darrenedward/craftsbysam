### D. Strategy Guidelines

#### Instagram Hashtag Strategy

**Per Post:** 25-30 hashtags total
- 5-8 core brand hashtags (always the same)
- 8-12 product-specific hashtags (rotate based on post content)
- 7-10 trending/discovery hashtags (mix of NZ-specific + craft community)

**Example Mix:**
```
#CraftsBySam #HandmadeNZ #NZMade #CustomCreations #HandmadeWithLove #SmallBusinessNZ #NZSmallBiz #CraftsBySamNZ #PersonalizedGlassware #CustomWineGlass #WineGlassNZ #BridalGlassware #WeddingGiftsNZ #BrideToBeNZ #WeddingEssentials #GiftIdeasNZ #PersonalizedGiftsNZ #BuyLocalNZ #SupportLocalNZ #NZMakers #MakerMovement #HandmadeBusiness #CraftyMum #WorkFromHomeMum #SmallBizLove #WeddingSeasonNZ
```

**Why This Works:**
- First 8 tags build consistent brand presence
- Middle tags target your specific product niche
- Last tags reach broader NZ and maker communities

---

#### Facebook Hashtag Strategy

**Per Post:** 3-5 hashtags maximum (Facebook's algorithm differs)

**Focus:** 2-3 core brand + 1-2 product-specific

**Example:**
```
#CraftsBySam #HandmadeNZ #WeddingGlassware
```

**Why Fewer on Facebook:**
- Facebook's audience responds better to minimal, targeted tagging
- Over-hashtagging can appear spammy
- Focus on 1-2 highly relevant tags per post

---

#### Rotation Schedule (Avoid "Shadowban" Risk)

**Don't use the exact same hashtag set every post.** Rotate product-specific and trending hashtags while keeping core brand tags consistent.

**Week 1 Mix:** Heavy on wedding/bridal tags
**Week 2 Mix:** Heavy on gift/general tags
**Week 3 Mix:** Heavy on NZ-made/local business tags
**Week 4 Mix:** Heavy on craft/maker community tags

**Repeat monthly with seasonal adjustments.**

---

#### Hashtag Placement Best Practices

**Instagram:**
- Place hashtags in the first comment (not caption) for cleaner aesthetic
- Or put them at the very bottom of the caption with line breaks (• • •)

**Facebook:**
- Integrate naturally into the post text or place at the end
- Keep minimal — 3-5 max

---

#### Hashtag Research & Updates

**Monthly:** Research trending hashtags in your niche using:
- Instagram's search suggestions (start typing #Crafts or #Wedding to see suggestions)
- Competitor analysis (see what similar NZ businesses are using)
- Seasonal additions (Christmas, Mother's Day, wedding season)

**Quarterly:** Audit your hashtag performance:
- Which hashtags bring the most engagement?
- Which hashtags are your ideal customers using?
- Remove underperforming hashtags and test new ones

## Brand Voice & Tone Guidelines

**Always:**
- Warm and personal ("I'm Sam," not "We are Crafts By Sam")
- Proud of craft and handmade quality
- Emphasize NZ-made and small business story
- Use emoji naturally but not excessively (2-4 per caption)

**Never:**
- Corporate or overly formal language
- Generic templated responses in DMs/comments
- Making promises about products you can't deliver
- Spammy hashtag behavior (30+ irrelevant tags)

**CTA Best Practices:**
- Clear, specific calls to action in every post
- Rotate CTAs: "Link in bio," "DM to order," "Comment below," "Tag a friend"
- Make it easy to take the next step
